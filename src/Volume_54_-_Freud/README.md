# Volume 54 - Freud

## SIGMUND FREUD (1856-1939)

Sigmund Freud was an Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis, a clinical method for treating mental illness through dialogue and a theory of the mind that emphasizes unconscious processes. His work transformed the understanding of human psychology and influenced art, literature, and culture.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Origin and Development of Psycho-Analysis** - Five lectures introducing the basic concepts of psychoanalysis, delivered in America in 1909.

- **Selected Papers on Hysteria** - Early clinical papers developing the psychoanalytic method.

- **The Sexual Enlightenment of Children** - Freud's views on childhood sexuality and education.

- **The Interpretation of Dreams** - Freud's groundbreaking work arguing that dreams are the "royal road to the unconscious," revealing repressed wishes through symbolic disguise.

- **On Narcissism** - Freud's analysis of self-love and its role in psychological development.

- **Instincts and Their Vicissitudes** - A theoretical paper on the nature and transformation of human drives.

- **Repression** - Freud's analysis of the mechanism by which unacceptable thoughts are excluded from consciousness.

- **The Unconscious** - A systematic account of the nature and operations of unconscious mental processes.

- **Observations on "Wild" Psycho-Analysis** - A critique of untrained or improper psychoanalytic practice.

- **The Future Prospects of Psycho-Analytic Therapy** - Reflections on the development and future of psychoanalytic treatment.

- **A General Introduction to Psycho-Analysis** - Freud's comprehensive introduction to his theories, based on lectures delivered 1915-1917.

- **Beyond the Pleasure Principle** - Freud's introduction of the death drive and revision of his drive theory.

- **Group Psychology and the Analysis of the Ego** - An analysis of group behavior and leadership through psychoanalytic concepts.

- **The Ego and the Id** - Freud's mature structural model of the mind, distinguishing id, ego, and superego.

- **Inhibitions, Symptoms, and Anxiety** - A revised theory of anxiety and its role in mental life.

- **Thoughts for the Times on War and Death** - Reflections on human aggression and mortality occasioned by World War I.

- **Civilization and Its Discontents** - Freud's analysis of the tensions between civilization and individual instinctual desires, one of his most widely read works.

- **New Introductory Lectures on Psycho-Analysis** - Later lectures updating and extending his earlier introduction.
