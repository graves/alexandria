# Volume 25 - Montaigne

## MICHEL DE MONTAIGNE (1533-1592)

Michel de Montaigne was a French Renaissance nobleman and the inventor of the essay as a literary form. Retiring from public life to his tower library, he devoted himself to self-examination and philosophical reflection, creating a new genre of personal literature.

### Works in This Volume

- **Essays** - A collection of personal reflections on an extraordinary range of subjects, from cannibals to coaches, from death to education, from friendship to solitude. Montaigne's method is digressive and exploratory, using classical quotations and personal anecdotes to test his ideas. His motto was "Que sais-je?" ("What do I know?"), and his essays embody a skeptical humanism that influenced thinkers from Shakespeare to Emerson. Notable essays include:
  - Of the Education of Children
  - Of Friendship
  - Of Cannibals
  - Of the Inconsistency of Our Actions
  - Of Experience
  - An Apology for Raymond Sebond
  - Of Solitude
  - Of Repentance
