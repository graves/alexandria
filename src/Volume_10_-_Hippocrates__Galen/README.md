# Volume 10 - Hippocrates, Galen

The foundational texts of Western medicine, spanning from the classical Greek period to the Roman era.

## HIPPOCRATES (c. 460-370 B.C.)

Known as the "Father of Medicine," Hippocrates established medicine as a rational discipline separate from philosophy and religion. The Hippocratic Corpus represents the collected medical writings of his school.

### Hippocratic Works
- **The Oath** - The ethical foundation of medical practice
- **The Law** - On the qualifications of a physician
- **On Ancient Medicine** - Defense of empirical medicine against philosophical speculation
- **On Airs, Waters, and Places** - Environmental medicine and epidemiology
- **The Book of Prognostics** - The art of medical prognosis
- **Aphorisms** - Concise medical wisdom and observations
- **Of the Epidemics** - Case studies in epidemic disease
- **On Regimen in Acute Diseases** - Treatment of acute illness through diet
- **On Injuries of the Head** - Traumatic head injuries and their treatment
- **On the Surgery** - Principles of surgical practice
- **On Fractures** - Treatment of broken bones
- **On the Articulations** - Joint injuries and dislocations
- **Instruments of Reduction** - Tools for treating fractures and dislocations
- **On Ulcers** - Treatment of wounds and ulcers
- **On Fistulae** - Treatment of fistulas
- **On Hemorrhoids** - Treatment of hemorrhoids
- **On the Sacred Disease** - Epilepsy explained as a natural, not divine, condition

## GALEN (c. 130-210 A.D.)

The most influential physician of the Roman Empire, Galen synthesized Greek medical knowledge and dominated Western medicine for over a millennium.

- **On the Natural Faculties** - The physiological functions of the body and critique of other medical theories
