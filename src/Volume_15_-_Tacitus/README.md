# Volume 15 - Tacitus

## TACITUS (c. 56-120 A.D.)

Publius Cornelius Tacitus was the greatest historian of the Roman Empire. His works are distinguished by their psychological insight, mordant wit, and penetrating analysis of power and its corruptions.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Annals** - A history of the Roman Empire from the death of Augustus to the death of Nero (14-68 A.D.), focusing on the reigns of Tiberius, Caligula, Claudius, and Nero. Tacitus exposes the moral decay of the imperial system and the loss of liberty under autocratic rule.

- **The Histories** - An account of the tumultuous "Year of the Four Emperors" (69 A.D.) and its aftermath, covering the civil wars following Nero's death and the rise of the Flavian dynasty. The surviving books cover the period from 69 to 70 A.D.
