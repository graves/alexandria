# Volume 39 - Adam Smith

## ADAM SMITH (1723-1790)

Adam Smith was a Scottish economist and philosopher, widely regarded as the father of modern economics. A leading figure of the Scottish Enlightenment, Smith's work laid the foundations for classical economics and the theory of free markets.

### Works in This Volume

- **An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations** - Smith's magnum opus, published in 1776, which revolutionized economic thought. Smith analyzes the division of labor, the function of markets, the role of self-interest, and the sources of national prosperity. His concept of the "invisible hand" guiding markets toward efficient outcomes became a cornerstone of economic theory.
