# Volume 12 - Lucretius, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius

Three masterworks of ancient philosophy addressing the nature of the universe and the art of living well.

## LUCRETIUS (c. 99-55 B.C.)

The great Roman poet-philosopher who expounded Epicurean philosophy in verse.

- **On the Nature of Things** (De Rerum Natura) - A poetic exposition of Epicurean physics and ethics, explaining the atomic structure of the universe, the mortality of the soul, and the path to tranquility through understanding nature

## EPICTETUS (c. 50-135 A.D.)

A former slave who became one of the most influential Stoic philosophers, teaching that freedom lies in accepting what we cannot control and mastering what we can.

- **The Discourses** - Lectures on Stoic philosophy as recorded by his student Arrian, covering virtue, freedom, and the proper use of impressions

## MARCUS AURELIUS (121-180 A.D.)

Roman Emperor and Stoic philosopher, Marcus Aurelius wrote his private philosophical reflections while campaigning on the empire's frontiers.

- **The Meditations** - Personal reflections on Stoic philosophy, duty, mortality, and living a virtuous life in the face of adversity
