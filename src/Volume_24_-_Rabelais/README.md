# Volume 24 - Rabelais

## FRANCOIS RABELAIS (c. 1494-1553)

Francois Rabelais was a French Renaissance writer, physician, and humanist. His satirical masterwork combines bawdy humor, wordplay, and profound humanist philosophy in a carnivalesque celebration of life.

### Works in This Volume

- **Gargantua and Pantagruel** - A sprawling comic epic following the adventures of two giants, Gargantua and his son Pantagruel. Through grotesque humor, parody, and satirical fantasy, Rabelais attacks religious hypocrisy, scholastic pedantry, and social pretension while celebrating the pleasures of the body, the joys of learning, and the freedom of the human spirit. The work includes:
  - The life of Gargantua and his education
  - The war against Picrochole
  - The founding of the Abbey of Theleme ("Do what you will")
  - The adventures of Pantagruel and his companion Panurge
  - The voyage to consult the Oracle of the Divine Bottle
