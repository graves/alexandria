# Volume 46 - Hegel

## GEORG WILHELM FRIEDRICH HEGEL (1770-1831)

Hegel was a German philosopher, one of the most influential figures in the history of Western thought. His dialectical method and comprehensive system of philosophy shaped subsequent developments in philosophy, politics, history, and social theory.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Philosophy of Right** - Hegel's mature political philosophy, tracing the development of ethical life through the family, civil society, and the state. He presents the rational state as the actualization of freedom and the ethical order as the reconciliation of individual will with universal principles.

- **The Philosophy of History** - Hegel's lectures on world history as the progress of the consciousness of freedom. He traces the dialectical development of Spirit through Oriental, Greek, Roman, and Germanic civilizations, seeing history as the rational unfolding of the Absolute.
