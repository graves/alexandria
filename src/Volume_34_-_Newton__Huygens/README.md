# Volume 34 - Newton, Huygens

## SIR ISAAC NEWTON (1642-1727)

Isaac Newton was an English mathematician, physicist, and astronomer, widely recognized as one of the most influential scientists of all time. His *Principia Mathematica* established classical mechanics, and his work on optics and calculus transformed our understanding of the physical world.

### Works in This Volume

- **Optics** - Newton's treatise on light and color, presenting his experiments with prisms and his corpuscular theory of light. The work includes his famous "Queries" speculating on the nature of light, gravity, and the structure of matter.

---

## CHRISTIAAN HUYGENS (1629-1695)

Huygens was a Dutch mathematician, physicist, and astronomer, one of the leading scientists of his era. He made fundamental contributions to probability theory, invented the pendulum clock, discovered Saturn's moon Titan, and developed the wave theory of light.

### Works in This Volume

- **Treatise on Light** - Huygens' exposition of his wave theory of light, explaining reflection and refraction through the propagation of waves in an ethereal medium. This work laid the foundation for the modern understanding of light as a wave phenomenon.
