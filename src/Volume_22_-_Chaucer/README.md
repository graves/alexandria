# Volume 22 - Chaucer

## GEOFFREY CHAUCER (c. 1343-1400)

Geoffrey Chaucer is the father of English literature and the greatest English poet of the Middle Ages. A diplomat, civil servant, and courtier, he brought Continental literary influences to England while creating a distinctive English voice.

### Works in This Volume

- **Troilus and Criseyde** - A tragic romance set during the Trojan War, telling the story of the Trojan prince Troilus and his love for Criseyde, who ultimately betrays him for the Greek warrior Diomede. A profound exploration of love, fate, and free will.

- **The Canterbury Tales** - An unfinished masterpiece in which a diverse group of pilgrims traveling to Canterbury Cathedral each tell stories to pass the time. The tales range from courtly romance to bawdy fabliau, creating a vivid panorama of medieval English society. Notable tales include:
  - The Knight's Tale
  - The Miller's Tale
  - The Wife of Bath's Tale
  - The Pardoner's Tale
  - The Nun's Priest's Tale
  - And many others...
