# Volume 07 - Plato

## PLATO (c. 428-348 B.C.)

Plato, student of Socrates and teacher of Aristotle, is one of the most influential philosophers in Western thought. His dialogues explore ethics, politics, metaphysics, epistemology, and aesthetics through dramatic conversations, usually featuring Socrates as the main speaker.

### Dialogues in This Volume

**Early Dialogues (Socratic)**
- **Apology** - Socrates' defense speech at his trial
- **Crito** - On the duty to obey the law
- **Euthyphro** - On the nature of piety
- **Charmides** - On temperance
- **Laches** - On courage
- **Lysis** - On friendship
- **Ion** - On poetic inspiration

**Transitional Dialogues**
- **Gorgias** - On rhetoric and justice
- **Meno** - On virtue and knowledge
- **Protagoras** - On the unity of virtue
- **Euthydemus** - On eristic argument

**Middle Dialogues**
- **The Republic** - On justice, the ideal state, and the nature of reality
- **Phaedo** - On the immortality of the soul
- **Symposium** - On the nature of love
- **Phaedrus** - On love, rhetoric, and the soul
- **Cratylus** - On the nature of language

**Late Dialogues**
- **Theaetetus** - On knowledge
- **Parmenides** - On the Forms and the One
- **Sophist** - On being and non-being
- **Statesman** - On the art of ruling
- **Philebus** - On pleasure and the good
- **Timaeus** - On cosmology and natural science
- **Critias** - On Atlantis (unfinished)
- **Laws** - On legislation and the second-best state

**Letters**
- **The Seventh Letter** - Plato's autobiographical account of his philosophical development
