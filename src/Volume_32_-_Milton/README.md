# Volume 32 - Milton

## JOHN MILTON (1608-1674)

John Milton was an English poet, polemicist, and civil servant, widely regarded as one of the greatest poets in the English language. A learned humanist fluent in Latin, Greek, Hebrew, and several modern languages, Milton served as Secretary for Foreign Tongues under Oliver Cromwell. He went blind in 1652 but continued his literary work, dictating his masterpiece *Paradise Lost* to his daughters.

### Works in This Volume

- **Paradise Lost** - Milton's epic poem in blank verse, recounting the Fall of Man: Satan's rebellion and expulsion from Heaven, the temptation of Adam and Eve, and humanity's loss of Paradise. A profound meditation on free will, obedience, and redemption.

- **Samson Agonistes** - A tragic drama modeled on Greek tragedy, depicting the final hours of the blind Samson among the Philistines. Written when Milton himself was blind, it explores themes of faith, suffering, and divine providence.

- **Areopagitica** - A passionate prose defense of the freedom of the press and against pre-publication censorship, addressed to Parliament. One of history's most influential arguments for free speech.

- **Miscellaneous Poems** - A collection including *Lycidas*, *L'Allegro*, *Il Penseroso*, *Comus*, and sonnets. These poems display Milton's mastery of classical and pastoral forms and his development as a poet.
