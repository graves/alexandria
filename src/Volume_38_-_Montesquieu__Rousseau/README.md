# Volume 38 - Montesquieu, Rousseau

## CHARLES DE SECONDAT, BARON DE MONTESQUIEU (1689-1755)

Montesquieu was a French judge, philosopher, and political theorist. His analysis of the separation of powers became a cornerstone of modern constitutional government and influenced the framers of the United States Constitution.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Spirit of Laws** - Montesquieu's masterwork of political philosophy, examining the relationship between laws and the physical, social, and political conditions of different societies. He develops the theory of separation of powers among legislative, executive, and judicial branches.

## JEAN-JACQUES ROUSSEAU (1712-1778)

Rousseau was a Genevan philosopher, writer, and composer whose ideas influenced the Enlightenment, the French Revolution, and modern political and educational thought. His work on social contract theory and natural education remains profoundly influential.

### Works in This Volume

- **A Discourse on the Origin of Inequality** - Rousseau's analysis of the origins of social inequality, arguing that civilization has corrupted natural human goodness. He traces how private property and social institutions created the chains that bind humanity.

- **The Social Contract** - Rousseau's treatise on political philosophy, opening with the famous line "Man is born free, and everywhere he is in chains." He develops the concept of the general will and popular sovereignty as the basis for legitimate government.
