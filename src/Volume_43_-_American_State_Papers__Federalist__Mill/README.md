# Volume 43 - American State Papers, Federalist, Mill

## AMERICAN STATE PAPERS

The foundational documents of American democracy, establishing the principles of self-government, individual rights, and federal union.

### Works in This Volume

- **Declaration of Independence** (1776) - Drafted primarily by Thomas Jefferson, this document proclaimed the American colonies' separation from Britain and articulated the philosophical foundations of American government: natural rights, popular sovereignty, and the right of revolution.

- **Articles of Confederation** (1781) - The first constitution of the United States, establishing a loose confederation of sovereign states. Its weaknesses led to the Constitutional Convention of 1787.

- **The Constitution of the United States** (1787) - The supreme law of the land, establishing the framework of federal government with its separation of powers, checks and balances, and protection of individual rights through the Bill of Rights.

---

## THE FEDERALIST (1787-1788)

A collection of 85 essays written by Alexander Hamilton, James Madison, and John Jay to promote ratification of the Constitution. These papers remain the most authoritative commentary on the American constitutional system.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Federalist** - The complete collection of Federalist essays, arguing for the ratification of the Constitution and explaining the principles of republican government, federalism, and the separation of powers.

---

## JOHN STUART MILL (1806-1873)

Mill was an English philosopher, political economist, and civil servant, one of the most influential liberal thinkers of the 19th century.

### Works in This Volume

- **Utilitarianism** - Mill's defense and refinement of utilitarian ethics, arguing that the greatest happiness principle provides the foundation for morality. He distinguishes between higher and lower pleasures and addresses objections to utilitarian theory.
