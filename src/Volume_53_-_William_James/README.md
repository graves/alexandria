# Volume 53 - William James

## WILLIAM JAMES (1842-1910)

William James was an American philosopher and psychologist, the leading figure of pragmatist philosophy and the father of American psychology. His work combined rigorous scientific inquiry with a humanistic concern for the richness of experience.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Principles of Psychology** - James's monumental work that established psychology as a scientific discipline in America. Combining physiological research with philosophical analysis, James explores consciousness, habit, attention, memory, emotion, will, and the self. His concept of the "stream of consciousness" proved influential in both psychology and literature.
