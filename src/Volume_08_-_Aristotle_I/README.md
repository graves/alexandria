# Volume 08 - Aristotle I

## ARISTOTLE (384-322 B.C.)

Aristotle, student of Plato and tutor to Alexander the Great, is one of the most influential philosophers in Western history. His works cover an extraordinary range of subjects, from logic and metaphysics to natural science and ethics. This volume contains his major works on logic, natural philosophy, metaphysics, and psychology.

### Works in This Volume

**The Organon (Logical Works)**
- **Categories** - Classification of the types of predication
- **On Interpretation** - Analysis of propositions and their truth conditions
- **Prior Analytics** - The theory of the syllogism and formal deductive reasoning
- **Posterior Analytics** - Scientific demonstration and the nature of knowledge
- **Topics** - Dialectical reasoning and the art of argumentation
- **Sophistical Refutations** - Fallacies and their detection

**Natural Philosophy**
- **Physics** - The principles of nature, motion, and change
- **On the Heavens** - Cosmology and the structure of the universe
- **On Generation and Corruption** - The principles of coming-to-be and passing-away
- **Meteorology** - Phenomena of the atmosphere and earth

**Metaphysics**
- **Metaphysics** - First philosophy: being, substance, and the unmoved mover

**Psychology (On the Soul)**
- **On the Soul** - The nature of the soul and its faculties
- **On Sense and the Sensible** - The nature of perception
- **On Memory and Reminiscence** - The workings of memory
- **On Sleep and Sleeplessness** - The physiology and purpose of sleep
- **On Dreams** - The nature and causes of dreams
- **On Prophesying by Dreams** - Whether dreams can predict the future
- **On Longevity and Shortness of Life** - Factors affecting lifespan
- **On Youth, Old Age, Life and Death, and Breathing** - The vital functions
