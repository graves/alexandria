# Volume 26 - Shakespeare I

## WILLIAM SHAKESPEARE (1564-1616)

William Shakespeare is the greatest writer in the English language and the world's preeminent dramatist. This volume contains his early comedies, histories, and the first tragedies.

### Works in This Volume

**Early Comedies**
- **The Comedy of Errors** - A farcical comedy of mistaken identity involving two sets of twins
- **The Two Gentlemen of Verona** - Friendship and love tested by romantic rivalry
- **The Taming of the Shrew** - Petruchio's wooing of the sharp-tongued Katharina
- **Love's Labour's Lost** - Nobles forswear love, only to fall for visiting ladies
- **A Midsummer Night's Dream** - Lovers, fairies, and mechanicals in an enchanted wood
- **The Merchant of Venice** - Antonio's bond with Shylock and Portia's wisdom
- **Much Ado About Nothing** - Beatrice and Benedick's witty warfare
- **As You Like It** - Rosalind in the Forest of Arden

**Histories**
- **The Life and Death of King John** - The reign of King John and its conflicts
- **The Tragedy of King Richard the Second** - Richard II's fall from power
- **The First Part of King Henry the Fourth** - Prince Hal, Falstaff, and Hotspur's rebellion
- **The Second Part of King Henry the Fourth** - Hal's journey toward kingship
- **The Life of King Henry the Fifth** - Henry V and the victory at Agincourt
- **The First Part of King Henry the Sixth** - England's losses in France
- **The Second Part of King Henry the Sixth** - The Wars of the Roses begin
- **The Third Part of King Henry the Sixth** - Civil war and Richard of Gloucester's rise
- **The Tragedy of King Richard the Third** - Richard III's bloody path to the throne

**Early Tragedies**
- **Titus Andronicus** - Revenge tragedy set in ancient Rome
- **Romeo and Juliet** - The immortal tragedy of star-crossed lovers
- **Julius Caesar** - The assassination of Caesar and its aftermath
