# Volume 30 - Francis Bacon

## FRANCIS BACON (1561-1626)

Francis Bacon, Baron Verulam and Viscount St. Albans, was an English philosopher, statesman, and essayist. He is celebrated as the prophet of the Scientific Revolution and the father of the empirical method.

### Works in This Volume

- **Advancement of Learning** - A survey and defense of all branches of human knowledge, arguing for the dignity of learning and proposing a comprehensive reform of the sciences. Bacon classifies the sciences according to the faculties of the mind: memory (history), imagination (poetry), and reason (philosophy).

- **Novum Organum** (The New Organon) - Bacon's masterwork on scientific method, intended to replace Aristotle's Organon. He identifies the "idols" (false notions) that obstruct understanding and proposes a new method of induction based on systematic observation and experiment to unlock nature's secrets.

- **New Atlantis** - An unfinished utopian fable describing the island of Bensalem and its research institution, Solomon's House. A prophetic vision of organized scientific research dedicated to improving human life.
