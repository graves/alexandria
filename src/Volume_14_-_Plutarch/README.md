# Volume 14 - Plutarch

## PLUTARCH (c. 46-120 A.D.)

Plutarch was a Greek biographer, essayist, and philosopher who lived during the Roman Empire. His biographical works became a major source for understanding the ancient world and profoundly influenced Western literature and political thought.

### Works in This Volume

- **The Lives of the Noble Grecians and Romans** (Parallel Lives) - A series of biographies pairing famous Greeks with comparable Romans to illuminate moral character and virtue. The Lives include:
  - Theseus and Romulus
  - Lycurgus and Numa
  - Solon and Publicola
  - Themistocles and Camillus
  - Pericles and Fabius Maximus
  - Alcibiades and Coriolanus
  - Timoleon and Aemilius Paulus
  - Pelopidas and Marcellus
  - Aristides and Cato the Elder
  - Philopoemen and Flamininus
  - Pyrrhus and Marius
  - Lysander and Sulla
  - Cimon and Lucullus
  - Nicias and Crassus
  - Sertorius and Eumenes
  - Agesilaus and Pompey
  - Alexander and Caesar
  - Phocion and Cato the Younger
  - Agis and Cleomenes, and the Gracchi
  - Demosthenes and Cicero
  - Demetrius and Antony
  - Dion and Brutus
  - And others...
